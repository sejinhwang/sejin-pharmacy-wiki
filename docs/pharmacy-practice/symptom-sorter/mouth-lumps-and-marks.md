# Mouth Lumps And Marks

## Overview

Many oral lumps and marks are benign or developmental anomalies, but the mouth is a high-risk site for squamous cell carcinoma.

The key task is to identify premalignant and malignant lesions early, especially leukoplakia, erythroplakia, indurated lesions, and persistent unexplained lumps.

## Differential Diagnosis

### Common

- Fibroepithelial polyp or traumatic fibroma
- Mucocele
- Papilloma
- Candidiasis
- Geographic tongue
- Torus palatinus or mandibular torus

### Occasional

- Lichen planus
- Leukoplakia
- Erythroplakia
- Pyogenic granuloma
- Amalgam tattoo

### Rare But Important

- Squamous cell carcinoma
- Salivary gland tumour
- Oral melanoma
- Kaposi sarcoma
- Pemphigus or pemphigoid

## Clinical Assessment

- Ask about duration, growth, pain, trauma, cheek or lip biting, smoking, alcohol, betel nut, immunosuppression, and systemic symptoms.
- Inspect colour: white, red, blue, pigmented, ulcerated, or mixed.
- Note site, especially high-risk sites such as lateral tongue and floor of mouth.
- Palpate consistency: soft, cystic, rubbery, hard, indurated, fixed, or tethered.
- Perform a scrape test when safe: candidiasis wipes off; leukoplakia, lichen planus, and hyperkeratosis do not.
- Examine neck nodes.

## Ready Reckoner

| Feature | Candidiasis | Lichen Planus | Leukoplakia | SCC | Mucocele |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Appearance | Curdy white | White lace | Adherent white plaque | Ulcer or mass | Blue cystic swelling |
| Wipes off | Yes | No | No | No | No |
| Pain | Soreness | Variable | Painless | Late | Painless |
| Typical site | Generalised | Buccal mucosa | Any | Tongue or floor | Lower lip |

## Investigations

- Swab is useful if candidiasis is suspected.
- Biopsy is essential for persistent unexplained lumps, leukoplakia, erythroplakia, indurated lesions, or cancer concern.

## Red Flags

- Unexplained lump for more than 3 weeks.
- Erythroplakia.
- Leukoplakia.
- Induration or fixation.
- Associated neck lump.
- Loose tooth not explained by periodontal disease.
- Ulcerated mass, lateral tongue lesion, floor of mouth lesion, or high-risk patient.

## Top Tips

- Leukoplakia is a clinical description, not a final diagnosis. It requires biopsy to exclude dysplasia or cancer.
- Torus palatinus is a benign bony midline hard-palate lump.
- Geographic tongue changes pattern over days or weeks and usually needs reassurance only.
