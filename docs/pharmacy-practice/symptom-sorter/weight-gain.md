# Weight Gain

## Overview

Weight gain is most commonly simple obesity, but assessment should separate fat gain from fluid retention, abdominal distension, pregnancy, endocrine disease, and medicine-related weight gain.

## Differential Diagnosis

### Common

- Simple obesity
- Hypothyroidism
- Pregnancy
- Oedema from heart, renal, or hepatic failure
- Alcohol excess

### Occasional

- Menopause
- Medicine-related weight gain
- PCOS
- Large ovarian cyst
- Activity restriction from physical or mental disability

### Rare But Important

- Anabolic steroid abuse
- Cushing's syndrome
- Hypothalamic lesion or hypopituitarism
- Insulinoma
- Genetic syndromes

## Clinical Assessment

- Ask about speed of gain, generalised versus abdominal gain, diet, activity, ankle swelling, breathlessness, orthopnoea, urine output, cold intolerance, constipation, dry skin, weakness, bruising, striae, mood change, menstrual history, pregnancy risk, alcohol, and medicines.
- Examine body habitus, oedema, ascites, raised JVP if assessed, abdomen, thyroid signs, and Cushingoid features.

## Ready Reckoner

| Feature | Obesity | Hypothyroidism | Pregnancy | Oedema | Alcohol |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Pitting oedema | Possible | No | Possible | Yes | Possible |
| Abdominal only | No | No | Yes | No | Possible |
| Long history | Yes | Possible | No | Possible | Possible |
| Palpable liver | No | No | No | Possible | Possible |
| Otherwise well | Yes | Possible | Possible | No | No |

## Investigations

- TFTs.
- Urinalysis, FBC, LFTs, U&E, pregnancy test, and pelvic ultrasound when indicated.
- ECG, CXR, BNP/NT-proBNP, and echocardiography pathway if heart failure is suspected.
- Specialist endocrine work-up for suspected Cushing's, insulinoma, or pituitary disease.

## Red Flags

- Diffuse weight gain with oedema or breathlessness.
- Predominantly abdominal swelling.
- New weight gain in an older adult.
- Any possibility of pregnancy.
- Unwell appearance or atypical history.

## Pharmacist Notes

- Review corticosteroids, insulin, sulfonylureas, antipsychotics, antidepressants, oestrogen therapy, and NSAID-related fluid retention.
- Correcting hypothyroidism may not fully reverse weight gain when obesity coexists.
