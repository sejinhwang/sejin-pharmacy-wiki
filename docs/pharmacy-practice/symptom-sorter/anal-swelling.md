# Anal Swelling

## Overview

Anal swelling is often embarrassing and may be mentioned late in a consultation. It is an examination-led diagnosis; do not assume it is haemorrhoids without inspection.

Perianal abscess and strangulated prolapsed haemorrhoids require urgent assessment.

## Differential Diagnosis

### Common

- Prolapsed haemorrhoid
- Perianal haematoma
- Skin tags
- Perianal abscess
- Rectal prolapse

### Occasional

- Warts
- Sebaceous cyst
- Sentinel pile
- Infected pilonidal sinus

### Rare But Important

- Hidradenitis suppurativa
- Anal fistula
- Anal or colorectal carcinoma

## Clinical Assessment

### History

- Onset and progression: sudden, long-standing, or rapidly progressive.
- Pain severity and effect on walking or sitting.
- Rectal bleeding or discharge.
- Constipation and straining.
- Fever or rigors.
- Diabetes, immunocompromise, IBD, anorectal trauma, or recent procedures.

### Examination

- Inspect for colour change, fluctuant swelling, pointing, bluish tense lump, prolapsed tissue, warts, and ulcerated lesions.
- Perform DRE when appropriate and tolerated.
- If pain limits examination, urgent referral is preferred when red flags are present.

## Investigations

- Usually none.
- Sexual health referral if warts suggest STI exposure.
- Biopsy in secondary care for suspicious lesions.
- Specialist work-up if Crohn's-related perianal disease is suspected.

## Red Flags

- Severe pain with difficulty sitting or walking.
- Fever or systemic upset.
- Very swollen and painful prolapsed haemorrhoid.
- Recurrent or multiple fistulae.
- Persistent ulcerating swelling or suspicious lesion.

## Management Notes

- The diagnosis is usually made by inspection.
- Swelling with discharge raises abscess, warts, prolapse, or fistula.
- Strangulation, abscess, malignancy concern, or uncontrolled pain should not be managed as routine haemorrhoids.

## Related Conditions

Haemorrhoids, perianal abscess, perianal Crohn's disease, anal fissure, malignancy, and rectal bleeding are the key linked pathways.
