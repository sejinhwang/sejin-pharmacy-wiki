# Macules

## Overview

A macule is a flat, well-demarcated area of altered colour. Larger flat lesions are often called patches.

Key decisions are colour, blanching, scale, acute versus chronic course, single versus multiple lesions, and medicine exposure.

## Differential Diagnosis

### Common

- Drug eruption
- Junctional naevus or flat mole
- Viral exanthem
- Freckles or solar lentigines
- Chloasma or melasma

### Occasional

- Measles or rubella
- Post-inflammatory pigmentation
- Cafe au lait macules
- Berloque dermatitis
- Vitiligo, pityriasis versicolor, or pityriasis alba

### Rare But Important

- Secondary syphilis
- Tuberculoid leprosy
- Typhoid rose spots
- Neurofibromatosis
- Lentigo maligna
- Peutz-Jeghers syndrome

## Clinical Assessment

- Ask about onset, itch, burning, infection symptoms, pregnancy, sun exposure, topical exposures, medicines, sexual risk, travel, and family history.
- Examine distribution, scale, blanching, mucosal involvement, irregular borders, variegation, and evolution.

## Ready Reckoner

| Feature | Drug eruption | Flat mole | Viral exanthem | Lentigines | Melasma |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Itch/burning | Often | No | Usually no | No | No |
| Variable pigment | No | Yes | No | Possible | Yes |
| Red | Yes | No | Yes | No | No |
| Small lesions | Variable | Possible | Variable | Common | Usually larger |
| Symmetric facial pattern | No | No | No | No | Yes |

## Investigations

- Usually clinical.
- Skin scrapings or Wood's lamp for pityriasis versicolor versus vitiligo.
- Rubella serology in pregnancy exposure.
- Syphilis serology if risk or unusual rash.
- Biopsy if malignancy is suspected.

## Red Flags

- Evolving pigmented lesion or lentigo maligna concern.
- Possible rubella in pregnancy.
- Mucocutaneous lentigines in a child.
- Non-blanching macules with systemic illness.

## Pharmacist Notes

- Build a medication timeline for suspected drug eruption.
- Ask about OTC and natural products.
- Review photosensitising medicines when pigment change follows sun exposure.
