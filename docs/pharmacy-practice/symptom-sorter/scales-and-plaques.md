# Scales And Plaques

## Overview

Scale reflects abnormal shedding from accelerated keratin turnover. A plaque is a raised lesion over 1 cm; many plaque conditions also scale.

Sort by distribution, border, symmetry, greasy versus dry scale, itch, and systemic illness.

## Differential Diagnosis

### Common

- Psoriasis
- Eczema or dermatitis
- Dermatophyte fungal infection
- Pityriasis versicolor
- Seborrhoeic dermatitis
- Seborrhoeic keratosis

### Occasional

- Lichen simplex
- Lichen planus
- Actinic keratosis
- Pityriasis rosea
- Juvenile plantar dermatosis
- Guttate psoriasis

### Rare But Important

- Bowen's disease
- Mycosis fungoides
- Drug-induced plaques
- Ichthyosis
- Secondary syphilis

## Clinical Assessment

- Ask about duration, recurrence, itch, distribution, exposures, contact, pets, medicines, systemic symptoms, joint symptoms, and nail changes.
- Examine morphology, distribution, nails, scalp, flexures, extensor surfaces, palms, soles, and suspicious solitary lesions.

## Ready Reckoner

| Feature | Psoriasis | Eczema | Fungal | Seborrhoeic dermatitis | Seborrhoeic keratosis |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Plaque | Yes | Possible | No | No | Possible |
| Well-demarcated | Yes | No | Often active edge | No | Yes |
| Markedly itchy | Variable | Often | Possible | Possible | No |
| Symmetrical | Often | Often | Usually no | Possible | No |
| Severe dandruff | Possible | Possible | Possible | Yes | No |

## Investigations

- Often none when classic.
- Wood's lamp or skin scraping for fungal disease.
- Patch testing for allergic contact dermatitis.
- Biopsy if diagnosis unclear or malignancy suspected.
- Syphilis serology if palms/soles and systemic features.

## Red Flags

- Erythroderma or near-universal redness and scale.
- Solitary enlarging scaly plaque on sun-exposed skin in an older adult.
- Pityriasis rosea-like rash on palms and soles with systemic symptoms.
- Systemic symptoms suggesting CTCL or malignancy.

## Pharmacist Notes

- Review beta-blockers, lithium, antimalarials, anticonvulsants, and topical steroid use.
- Avoid high-potency steroid use on face or genitals unless directed.
- Screen psoriasis patients for nail disease and joint symptoms.
