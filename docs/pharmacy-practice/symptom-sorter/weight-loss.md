# Weight Loss

## Overview

Weight loss should be taken seriously. Confirm true weight loss using documented weights when possible, quantify the time course, and assess for systemic, respiratory, gastrointestinal, endocrine, mood, eating-disorder, and malignancy features.

## Differential Diagnosis

### Common

- Stress-related non-pathological weight loss
- Major depressive disorder
- Eating and feeding disorders
- Hyperthyroidism or excess thyroid replacement
- Malignancy

### Occasional

- Anxiety
- Terminal illness
- Substance misuse
- Cardiac, renal, or hepatic failure
- Undiagnosed diabetes
- Chronic inflammatory disease
- GI disease such as IBD, coeliac disease, ulcer disease, or parasites

### Rare But Important

- Tuberculosis
- Addison's disease
- Phaeochromocytoma
- HIV/AIDS
- Malnutrition

## Clinical Assessment

- Quantify amount and time period.
- Ask about appetite, intake, dysphagia, early satiety, fever, night sweats, fatigue, cough, haemoptysis, dyspnoea, diarrhoea, blood in stool, abdominal pain, heat intolerance, tremor, palpitations, polyuria, polydipsia, mood, stress, restrictive eating, purging, exercise, alcohol, substances, and laxatives.
- Examine general appearance, hydration, vitals, oral cavity, lymph nodes, chest, abdomen, hepatosplenomegaly, and safeguarding concerns in children.

## Ready Reckoner

| Feature | Stress | Depression | Eating disorder | Hyperthyroidism | Malignancy |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Mild anxiety | Yes | Possible | No | No | No |
| Loss of appetite | Possible | Yes | No | No | Yes |
| Distorted body image | No | No | Yes | No | No |
| Recurrent | Yes | Possible | Possible | Possible | No |
| Severe malaise | No | Yes | No | Possible | Yes |

## Investigations

- Urinalysis, FBC, ESR/CRP, TFTs, U&E, LFTs, CXR, and glucose or HbA1c.
- Autoimmune screen, HIV testing, faecal calprotectin, coeliac serology, stool studies, or further imaging according to symptoms.

## Red Flags

- Rapid weight loss with malaise and respiratory or GI symptoms.
- Eating disorder concern in a young person.
- Weight loss in a child.
- Cachectic appearance.
- Depression with significant risk.

## Pharmacist Notes

- Review stimulants, nausea-causing medicines, appetite-suppressing medicines, excess thyroid replacement, alcohol, substances, and laxative misuse.
- Address nutrition and hydration risk while investigations proceed.
