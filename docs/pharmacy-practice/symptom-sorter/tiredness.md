# Tiredness

## Overview

Feeling tired all the time is common. Clarify whether the patient means sleepiness, low mood, weakness, reduced exercise tolerance, or breathlessness.

The aim is to identify common causes while not missing weight loss, fever, night sweats, progressive decline, or focal neurological symptoms.

## Differential Diagnosis

### Common

- Depression
- Stress, overwork, boredom, or young children
- Anaemia
- Post-viral fatigue
- Hypothyroidism

### Occasional

- Diabetes mellitus
- Iron deficiency
- Coeliac disease
- Insomnia
- Chronic fatigue syndrome
- Heart, liver, or kidney failure
- Hyperthyroidism
- Substance misuse
- Medicine-related tiredness

### Rare But Important

- Malignancy
- Chronic infection, including TB, hepatitis, and HIV
- Neurological disease
- Addison's disease or electrolyte disorder
- Connective tissue disease
- Carbon monoxide poisoning

## Clinical Assessment

- Clarify symptom type, duration, sleep, mood, stressors, fever, night sweats, weight change, pain, dyspnoea, oedema, palpitations, endocrine symptoms, medicines, substances, and household exposure.
- Examine general appearance, vitals, pallor, lymph nodes, chest, cardiovascular system, abdomen, and focused neurology when indicated.

## Ready Reckoner

| Feature | Depression | Stress | Anaemia | Post-viral | Hypothyroidism |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Diurnal variation | Yes | Possible | No | No | No |
| Life event trigger | Possible | Yes | No | No | No |
| Recent illness | Possible | Possible | Possible | Yes | No |
| Pallor | No | No | Yes | No | Possible |
| Cold dry skin | No | No | Possible | No | Yes |

## Investigations

- Urinalysis, FBC, glucose or HbA1c, and TFTs.
- ESR/CRP, U&E, LFTs, ferritin, calcium, and coeliac serology according to context.
- CXR, autoimmune testing, HIV, EBV, hepatitis, or other infection testing when guided by history.

## Red Flags

- Tiredness with weight loss.
- Persistent fever, night sweats, or progressive decline.
- Abnormal pulse, pallor, lymph nodes, chest, or abdominal findings.
- Patient begins to look unwell.
- Depression with self-harm or suicide risk.

## Pharmacist Notes

- Review beta-blockers, diuretics, sedatives, polypharmacy, anticholinergic burden, alcohol, and substance use.
- Consider NSAIDs, anticoagulants, and antiplatelets when anaemia or occult bleeding is possible.
