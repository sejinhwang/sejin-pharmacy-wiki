# Swollen Glands

## Overview

Swollen glands, or lymphadenopathy, are often reactive but require systematic assessment. Decide whether nodes are regional or generalised, acute or persistent, tender or hard/fixed, and whether systemic symptoms are present.

## Differential Diagnosis

### Common

- Local infection, including URTI or tonsillitis
- Generalised viral infection such as glandular fever
- Metastatic malignancy
- Haematological malignancy
- Sepsis

### Occasional

- Sarcoidosis
- Cat scratch disease
- Measles or rubella
- SLE or rheumatoid arthritis
- Lymphogranuloma venereum or granuloma inguinale

### Rare But Important

- Syphilis
- HIV/AIDS
- Tuberculosis
- Tropical infections
- Medicine reactions

## Clinical Assessment

- Ask about duration, distribution, tenderness, alcohol-related pain, URTI, dental symptoms, skin infection, fever, night sweats, weight loss, fatigue, travel, cat scratches, sexual risk, TB exposure, and medicines.
- Examine node size, tenderness, mobility, firmness, fixation, matted nodes, distribution, ENT/oral cavity, skin, and abdomen for hepatosplenomegaly.

## Ready Reckoner

| Feature | Local infection | Viral | Metastases | Haematological malignancy | Sepsis |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Tender nodes | Yes | Yes | No | Possible | Possible |
| Persistent over 6 weeks | No | No | Yes | Yes | No |
| Regional only | Yes | No | Yes | Possible | No |
| Splenomegaly | No | Possible | No | Possible | No |
| Night sweats | No | Possible | Possible | Yes | Possible |

## Investigations

- None when clearly localised and self-limiting.
- FBC and ESR/CRP if generalised or persistent.
- Heterophile antibody test, CXR, targeted serology, HIV testing, syphilis testing, or Bartonella testing according to context.
- Lymph node biopsy or CT through specialist pathway when persistent or malignant features are present.

## Red Flags

- Persistent cervical lymphadenopathy in middle-aged or older adults.
- Left supraclavicular node with weight loss.
- Generalised persistent lymphadenopathy with weight loss or night sweats.
- Slowly enlarging non-tender node in unusual site.
- Hard, fixed, matted nodes.

## Pharmacist Notes

- Review phenytoin, antibiotics, sulphonamides, and recent medicine changes.
- Avoid repeated empirical antibiotics when systemic features or malignant features are present.
- Lower threshold for investigation in immunocompromised patients.
