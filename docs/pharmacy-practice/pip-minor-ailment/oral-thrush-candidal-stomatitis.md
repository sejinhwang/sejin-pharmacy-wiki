# Oral Thrush Candidal Stomatitis

Status: Public-safe standalone draft. Requires clinical/local policy review before use as a protocol.

## Scope

Oral thrush, or candidal stomatitis, is a superficial Candida infection of the oral mucosa. It may present with white plaques, erythema, soreness, altered taste, angular fissuring, or discomfort with eating and drinking.

This page is intended as a public clinical reference and migration draft. It does not include patient identifiers, personal prescriber identifiers, signatures, registration numbers, or private workflow credentials.

## Assessment Framework

### Presenting Symptoms

Document:

- white plaques, erythema, soreness, burning, altered taste, or dry mouth
- whether plaques can be wiped off and whether mucosa bleeds or is erythematous underneath
- location: tongue, buccal mucosa, palate, gums, lips, or angles of mouth
- duration and recurrence
- dysphagia, odynophagia, or retrosternal discomfort
- denture use and denture hygiene
- inhaled corticosteroid use and mouth-rinsing technique
- recent antibiotic use
- oral intake and hydration impact

### Patient Context

Assess:

- age and frailty
- pregnancy or breastfeeding status where treatment choice may be affected
- diabetes or glycaemic control concerns
- immunocompromised status, chemotherapy, transplant medicines, HIV risk, or systemic corticosteroids
- xerostomia, Sjogren's syndrome, radiotherapy history, or salivary issues
- current medicines including inhaled corticosteroids, antibiotics, immunosuppressants, and xerostomia-causing medicines
- allergy status and previous adverse reactions
- previous treatment response and recurrence pattern

## Red Flags Requiring Referral

Refer for medical assessment if any of the following are present:

- dysphagia, odynophagia, or suspected oesophageal candidiasis
- immunocompromised status with new or worsening oral candidiasis
- recurrent or persistent thrush despite appropriate measures
- unexplained weight loss, systemic symptoms, or significant malaise
- severe pain, inability to maintain oral intake, or dehydration risk
- suspected oral malignancy, leukoplakia, lichen planus, or other non-candidal lesion
- lesions that cannot be wiped off or atypical oral lesions
- infant, frail older adult, or high-risk patient with concerning symptoms according to local pathway

Escalate urgently if airway compromise, severe dehydration, or severe systemic illness is suspected.

## Focused Assessment / Observations

When available and within scope, document:

| Finding | Relevance |
| --- | --- |
| Plaque appearance | Wipeable white plaques support candidiasis; fixed lesions require caution. |
| Oral erythema/soreness | May indicate erythematous candidiasis or denture stomatitis. |
| Denture status | Poor denture hygiene or overnight wear may contribute. |
| Inhaler technique | ICS use without rinsing increases risk. |
| Swallowing symptoms | Dysphagia/odynophagia may suggest oesophageal involvement. |
| Immune/diabetes status | Raises recurrence and complication risk. |

## Differential Diagnosis

Consider:

- oral candidiasis
- aphthous ulcers
- leukoplakia
- oral lichen planus
- traumatic ulcer or frictional keratosis
- oral herpes or other viral lesions
- geographic tongue
- xerostomia-related irritation
- oral malignancy if persistent, fixed, ulcerated, or atypical

## Management Principles

### Non-Pharmacological Measures

Advise where appropriate:

- review denture hygiene and remove dentures overnight where appropriate
- clean dentures regularly and assess fit
- rinse mouth after inhaled corticosteroid use
- review inhaler technique and spacer use where appropriate
- optimise oral hygiene
- address dry mouth triggers where possible
- review recent antibiotics or medicines contributing to xerostomia where relevant

### Medicines / Product Selection

Treatment selection should follow local policy, formulary, product monograph, patient-specific contraindications, age restrictions, pregnancy/breastfeeding status, hepatic/renal function, immune status, interacting medicines, prior response, and scope of practice.

General public-facing approach:

| Presentation | Practical option |
| --- | --- |
| Mild typical oral thrush without red flags | Consider local topical treatment pathway where appropriate. |
| Denture-associated symptoms | Include denture hygiene and denture review. |
| ICS-associated symptoms | Reinforce mouth rinsing and inhaler technique. |
| Recurrent or refractory symptoms | Refer for clinician review and risk-factor assessment. |
| Dysphagia or immunocompromised status | Refer for medical assessment. |

Detailed product- or dose-specific tables should be reviewed against current local policy before inclusion in this public wiki.

## Counselling Points

Counsel on:

- oral hygiene and denture hygiene
- completing any selected treatment according to local/product instructions
- avoiding sharing oral appliances
- rinsing after inhaled corticosteroid use
- reassessment if symptoms persist or recur
- seeking review for swallowing difficulty, severe pain, or systemic symptoms
- checking for medicine interactions if systemic therapy is considered by a clinician

## Safety-Netting

Advise the patient to seek medical attention if:

- swallowing pain or difficulty develops
- symptoms worsen or do not improve as expected
- oral intake becomes difficult
- lesions are fixed, ulcerated, bleeding, or atypical
- thrush recurs repeatedly
- the patient is immunocompromised or has poorly controlled diabetes with worsening symptoms

## Documentation Template

Use local documentation workflows. A public-safe note may include:

**Assessment:** Oral symptoms consistent with possible candidal stomatitis: location [x], appearance [white plaques/erythema/soreness], duration [x]. Red flags screened: dysphagia/odynophagia, atypical fixed lesions, systemic symptoms [summary]. Risk factors reviewed: dentures, ICS use, antibiotics, diabetes, immune status.

**Impression:** Likely oral candidiasis / alternative diagnosis considered. Referral required: [yes/no and reason].

**Plan:** Oral hygiene, denture/inhaler counselling, and risk-factor advice provided. Product/medicine advice, if any, selected according to local policy and patient-specific factors. Safety-netting and follow-up advice provided.
