# Uncomplicated Cystitis

Status: Public-safe standalone draft. Requires clinical/local policy review before use as a protocol.

## Scope

Uncomplicated cystitis refers to lower urinary tract symptoms consistent with bladder infection in an otherwise lower-risk patient, without features suggesting pyelonephritis, complicated UTI, pregnancy-related risk, systemic illness, or other diagnoses requiring referral.

This page is intended as a public clinical reference and migration draft. It does not include patient identifiers, personal prescriber identifiers, signatures, registration numbers, or private workflow credentials.

## Assessment Framework

### Presenting Symptoms

Document:

- dysuria
- urinary frequency or urgency
- suprapubic discomfort
- haematuria, if present
- symptom onset and duration
- fever, chills, flank pain, nausea/vomiting, or systemic symptoms
- vaginal/urethral discharge, pelvic pain, or STI risk where relevant
- recurrent UTI history
- recent antibiotic exposure
- recent urinary instrumentation or catheter use

### Patient Context

Assess:

- age and frailty
- pregnancy or possible pregnancy
- sex assigned at birth and relevant anatomy where clinically appropriate
- renal impairment or known urinary tract abnormality
- diabetes, immunocompromised status, kidney stones, or recurrent pyelonephritis
- current medicines and allergies
- previous urine culture results and resistance history if available
- previous treatment response and adverse reactions
- ability to maintain hydration and access care if worsening

## Red Flags Requiring Referral

Refer for medical assessment if any of the following are present:

- fever, chills, rigors, or systemic illness
- flank pain or suspected pyelonephritis
- nausea/vomiting preventing oral intake
- pregnancy or possible pregnancy according to local policy
- male urinary symptoms where local minor ailment scope does not apply
- catheter-associated symptoms or recent urinary instrumentation
- recurrent, persistent, or treatment-refractory symptoms
- known structural urinary tract abnormality, kidney stones, or significant renal disease
- immunocompromised status with worsening symptoms
- gross haematuria or haematuria persisting after symptom resolution
- symptoms suggesting STI, vaginitis, pelvic inflammatory disease, or other gynaecological/urological diagnosis

Escalate urgently if sepsis, pyelonephritis, acute obstruction, or severe systemic illness is suspected.

## Focused Assessment / Observations

When available and within scope, document:

| Finding | Relevance |
| --- | --- |
| Temp | Fever suggests upper UTI/systemic infection. |
| Flank pain/CVA tenderness | Raises concern for pyelonephritis. |
| Suprapubic discomfort | Supports lower urinary tract involvement. |
| Hydration and oral intake | Poor intake/vomiting increases risk and may require referral. |
| Pregnancy status | Changes assessment and treatment pathway. |
| Recurrent history | May require culture, investigation, or clinician review. |

## Differential Diagnosis

Consider:

- uncomplicated cystitis
- pyelonephritis
- STI-related urethritis or cervicitis
- vaginitis
- pelvic inflammatory disease
- nephrolithiasis
- interstitial cystitis/bladder pain syndrome
- medication- or irritant-related urinary symptoms
- genitourinary syndrome of menopause where relevant

## Management Principles

### Non-Pharmacological Measures

Advise where appropriate:

- maintain adequate fluid intake without overhydration
- avoid delaying urination if symptomatic
- consider analgesia options according to local policy and patient-specific risk
- seek reassessment promptly if symptoms worsen or systemic features develop

### Medicines / Product Selection

Treatment selection should follow local policy, formulary, resistance patterns, product monograph, patient-specific contraindications, pregnancy/breastfeeding status, renal function, allergy history, prior cultures, previous response, and scope of practice.

General public-facing approach:

| Presentation | Practical option |
| --- | --- |
| Typical lower UTI symptoms without red flags | Consider local uncomplicated cystitis pathway where eligible. |
| Fever, flank pain, vomiting, or systemic illness | Refer for urgent assessment. |
| Pregnancy or possible pregnancy | Follow pregnancy-specific local pathway or refer. |
| Recurrent or persistent symptoms | Consider culture/clinician review according to local policy. |
| STI/vaginal symptoms | Refer or direct to appropriate sexual health/clinical pathway. |

Detailed product- or dose-specific tables should be reviewed against current local policy before inclusion in this public wiki.

## Counselling Points

Counsel on:

- expected symptom improvement timeframe according to selected management plan
- completing any selected treatment as directed by local policy/product instructions
- hydration and comfort measures
- avoiding duplicate or inappropriate OTC products
- red flags requiring urgent assessment
- seeking reassessment if symptoms persist, recur, or worsen
- adverse effect monitoring for any selected medicine according to product information

## Safety-Netting

Advise the patient to seek medical attention if:

- fever, chills, flank pain, vomiting, or systemic illness develops
- symptoms worsen or do not improve within the expected timeframe
- haematuria persists after symptoms resolve
- recurrent symptoms occur soon after treatment
- pregnancy is possible or confirmed
- severe pain, confusion, dehydration, or acute deterioration occurs
