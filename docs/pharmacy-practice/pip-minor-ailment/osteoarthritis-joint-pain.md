# Osteoarthritis Joint Pain

Status: Public-safe standalone draft. Requires clinical/local policy review before use as a protocol.

## Scope

Osteoarthritis (OA) joint pain is a common chronic musculoskeletal presentation. This page focuses on lower-risk, non-acute OA-type pain where red flags, inflammatory arthritis, trauma, infection, and urgent referral features have been excluded.

This page is intended as a public clinical reference and migration draft. It does not include patient identifiers, personal prescriber identifiers, signatures, registration numbers, or private workflow credentials.

## Assessment Framework

### Presenting Symptoms

Document:

- affected joint or joints
- pain duration and pattern
- stiffness, including morning stiffness duration
- swelling, warmth, erythema, locking, instability, or reduced ROM
- functional impact: walking, stairs, grip, sleep, work, ADLs
- triggers and relieving factors
- previous diagnosis of OA or imaging history if known
- recent trauma, fall, overuse, or change in activity
- previous treatments tried and response

### Patient Context

Assess:

- age and frailty
- pregnancy or breastfeeding status where treatment choice may be affected
- renal disease, HF, hypertension, GI ulcer/bleeding risk, liver disease, anticoagulant/antiplatelet use, or CV risk
- diabetes, immunocompromised status, or infection risk
- current medicines including NSAIDs, ASA, anticoagulants, corticosteroids, and OTC analgesics
- allergy status and previous adverse reactions
- falls risk and mobility aids
- goals of care and patient priorities

## Red Flags Requiring Referral

Refer for medical assessment if any of the following are present:

- acute trauma, suspected fracture, dislocation, tendon rupture, or inability to weight bear
- hot, red, swollen joint or suspected septic arthritis
- fever or systemic illness
- sudden severe joint pain, especially with swelling
- suspected gout, inflammatory arthritis, or crystal arthritis requiring diagnosis
- neurological deficit, severe weakness, or new bowel/bladder symptoms if back/hip-related
- unexplained weight loss, night pain, or malignancy concern
- rapidly worsening pain, deformity, or loss of function
- significant medication safety risk limiting OTC/self-care options

Escalate to A&E if septic arthritis, fracture/dislocation, acute neurovascular compromise, or severe systemic illness is suspected.

## Focused Assessment / Observations

When available and within scope, document:

| Finding | Relevance |
| --- | --- |
| Joint location and function | Helps distinguish OA pattern from acute injury or inflammatory disease. |
| Warmth/erythema/swelling | Red flags for infection, crystal arthritis, or inflammatory arthritis. |
| ROM and weight-bearing ability | Marked limitation or inability to weight bear requires referral. |
| Pain pattern | OA often worsens with use and improves with rest; inflammatory patterns require caution. |
| Medicine risk profile | NSAID/analgesic choices depend on renal, GI, CV, anticoagulant, and frailty risk. |

## Differential Diagnosis

Consider:

- osteoarthritis
- acute sprain/strain or overuse injury
- gout or pseudogout
- rheumatoid arthritis or other inflammatory arthritis
- septic arthritis
- bursitis or tendinopathy
- fracture or insufficiency fracture
- referred pain from spine/hip/knee
- neuropathic pain or radiculopathy

## Management Principles

### Non-Pharmacological Measures

Advise where appropriate:

- activity pacing and joint protection
- low-impact exercise and strengthening
- weight management where relevant and patient-centred
- heat or cold therapy according to preference and symptom pattern
- supportive footwear or assistive devices where appropriate
- physiotherapy, OT, or exercise programme referral where available
- falls-risk review for frail patients or those with mobility impairment

### Medicines / Product Selection

Treatment selection should follow local policy, formulary, product monograph, patient-specific contraindications, age, frailty, pregnancy/breastfeeding status, renal function, GI/CV risk, anticoagulant/antiplatelet use, prior response, and scope of practice.

General public-facing approach:

| Presentation | Practical option |
| --- | --- |
| Mild intermittent OA-type pain | Consider non-pharmacological strategies and simple analgesic approach according to local policy. |
| Localised superficial joint pain | Consider topical therapy where appropriate and safe. |
| Significant renal/GI/CV/anticoagulant risk | Avoid routine NSAID self-management; consider clinician review. |
| Function-limiting chronic pain | Encourage MDT/non-pharmacological supports and clinician review. |
| Acute hot/swollen joint or systemic symptoms | Refer urgently. |

Detailed product- or dose-specific tables should be reviewed against current local policy before inclusion in this public wiki.

## Counselling Points

Counsel on:

- realistic goals: pain reduction, function improvement, and flare prevention
- using non-pharmacological measures consistently
- avoiding duplicate analgesic ingredients across OTC products
- avoiding prolonged OTC NSAID use without review, especially in renal/GI/CV risk or anticoagulant use
- monitoring for GI bleeding symptoms, oedema, SOB, BP worsening, or renal-risk situations if relevant
- seeking reassessment if pain pattern changes or red flags develop

## Safety-Netting

Advise the patient to seek medical attention if:

- the joint becomes hot, red, very swollen, or severely painful
- fever or systemic illness develops
- trauma occurs or weight-bearing becomes difficult/impossible
- neurological symptoms develop
- pain worsens rapidly or becomes persistent nocte/rest pain
- OTC or self-care measures are ineffective or unsafe due to comorbidities/medicines

## Documentation Template

Use local documentation workflows. A public-safe note may include:

**Assessment:** OA-type joint pain affecting [joint], duration [x], functional impact [x]. Red flags screened: hot/swollen joint, trauma, fever/systemic symptoms, inability to weight bear, neuro symptoms [summary]. PMHx and medicine risk profile reviewed, including renal/GI/CV and anticoagulant risk.

**Impression:** Likely OA-related joint pain / alternative diagnosis considered. Referral required: [yes/no and reason].

**Plan:** Non-pharmacological advice provided. Product/medicine advice, if any, selected according to local policy and patient-specific risk factors. Safety-netting and follow-up advice provided.
